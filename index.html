Learn About Finance

Asset vs Liability

Assets put money in your pocket - things you own that have value (savings, investments, property).

Liabilities take money out - things you owe (loans, credit card debt, mortgage).

Building assets and reducing liabilities increases your net worth!

Investments

Putting money to work to grow over time. Common investments include stocks, bonds, and real estate.

Key concepts: Diversification, compound interest, risk vs reward.

Historically, the stock market averages 7-10% returns per year!

Budgeting

A plan for your money. The 50/30/20 rule:

• 50% - Needs (rent, food, utilities)

• 30% - Wants (entertainment, dining out)

• 20% - Savings & debt repayment

Track expenses to find where your money really goes!

Debt Management

Strategies to handle what you owe:

Snowball method: Pay smallest debts first for quick wins.

Avalanche method: Pay highest interest debts first to save money.

Good debt (mortgage) vs Bad debt (high-interest credit cards)
